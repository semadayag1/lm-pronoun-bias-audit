High school was...for a lack of better words, “rough”, I was a terrible student, not because i am bad at studying, but rather because I feel like I just perform better in practice than in theory. Yet high school where i came from was all theory, rigorous exams, rote memorization, constant studying, it took a toll on every soul in my class. In India, the idea is, “if you pushed enough information into a kids head hard enough it should fit right?”, the answer is no, no it doesn't, it pops right out the second you stop pushing and that's what happened to me, my memory would be constantly overloaded and by the time i reached the exam hall, it had already pushed everything out and I sat there with near to no recollection of whatever i ran through my head last night. Moving to cpp, most of my classes, i hoped would have less a emphasis on exams and more on practical work, projects, assignments and presentations, its “learn by doing” motto was something i thought id feel at home in. I'm not completely sure, but it seems to me that coming in i believed that this sudden shift from pure theory to heavy practice would crack open the cork blocking the bottleneck that kept me from exploding with my full potential, yet i was hit with the brutal reality, exams refuse to leave me, all my classes had final exams. Maybe I just am better at applying things I learn than theoretical gibberish, and that my calling is in the applied sciences, not theoretical yet unfortunately I have found myself in a situation that pushes me deeper into theory, not only am I still struggling to deal with exams, the fact that I have had to step into the world as an adult, something still rather new to me, and the weight of responsibilities that come with it are bearing down on me. Now i miss school, atleast it wasnt this much pressure and stress to not only do well in school but also survive on your own, rebuild your social life, be active and work to build toward a job and all the other adulting challenges and yet here I am still wondering how I will pass midterms.
