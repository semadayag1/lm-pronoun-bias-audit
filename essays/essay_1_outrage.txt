"In today's world, AI is as common place as is a fridge or a microwave, it has become a everyday appliance, yet unlike everyday appliance which are specialized toward one activity, AI seems to be able to do almost anything, which includes, and is not limited to, my homework. Every student in my class uses AI, and as someone who cares for my academic integrity, it is absolutely ridiculous that someone who hasn't put in half the effort I have manually doing all my work, scores above me simply through tools they had no contribution in even building. Assignments have become trivial, take-home tests redundant, back in the day when we hit a roadblock, we would have to run through textbooks or ask an adult for help and learn to do it right. Now we just ask AI to do it for us. I am exhausted by the fact that at times it feels like even some faculty are simply using AI to generate coursework or assignments. We must immediately stop this lest even the ones who still choose to lift a pen decide that in order to stay level with their peers, they must pick up their phone instead. I fear a day when we are all regurgitating AI slop back and forth and creativity dies alongside it, where hard work is replaced by prompt engineering and knowledge by easy access to information. AI is a tool, and should be treated as such, not as our arms and legs. Hence, why I fully support the ban of AI at university level and condemn the use of it for anything beyond a tool to assist academic endeavors."
