SAT, every student’s nuisance and annoyance, like as if studying for exams and getting good grades wasn't enough, we also have to take standardized testing, after the fact that our high schools grades themselves are verified by schools for being “of standard” themselves, schools won't accept unverified grades anyway, leaving this entire system practically redundant. It has become more of a money grab than a merit to determine a student's capacity and merit. People claim, “Oh it helps establish an equalized measure of every student's ability across the same baseline”, yet the counter argument completely fails to realize is all it measures is a student's capacity to prepare for a test, the wealth of resources at their disposal and nothing else, heck give me enough money and I am sure I could have cracked it with a perfect grade with all the coaching, practice tests, cheat sheets, questions banks in the world at my disposal. It is an undeniable fact that no student truly likes the SAT; they do it simply because colleges force them to and to stay competitive; they have no choice, and anyone can see that given the option not to, they almost never will. It also adds no real value beyond what is already taught in school, infact its inferior, and most often, students that come from non-English or math based high school backgrounds, not everyone is good at English or math but could be excellent at let's say chemistry. It's a monopoly with no accountability, redundant and outdated, and skewed heavily in favor of wealthy individuals. I find myself having little to no respect for the College Board and ACT establishment and find SAT’s removal to only be of benefit to future generations.
