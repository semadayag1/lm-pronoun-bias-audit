When we think of social media, we think “ouhhh cat videos”, but what exactly is it underneath that's giving you more and more cat videos? Simple, it's math. Math designed to keep you scrolling, math that knows what you like, math that's designed to keep you coming back for more. The algorithm we are talking about here is called collaborative filtering, which doesn't actually analyze the content of the video itself, but rather analyses you, the user behavior. The system takes millions of users and millions of videos and calculates the mathematical similarities between their watch histories, what this does is create a matrix where every user is mapped against every item to predict what they will click next based on the behavior of their "nearest neighbors." Which is precisely why my entire recommendation feed is completely ruined at the moment because my roommate decided to “borrow” my account last weekend and watch forty-five straight minutes of competitive dog grooming videos. I don't even like dogs but now my entire feed has boiled down to who can color their poodle the oddest color of the rainbow, or worse...the entire rainbow. I find myself needing to manually click “not interested” every time one of those hairy k-nines comes across my screen just so I could fix my recommendations weights, which is rather infuriating because they have their own phone which is also filled with dog videos. With that said, by calculating the dot product of the user vector and the item vector, the system outputs a prediction score for videos you haven't seen yet and If that score passes a certain threshold, it gets pushed to the top of your feed, guaranteeing maximum retention and engagement, which is exactly how this algorithm takes over your life.
